Instream Spawning and Rearing Habitat

Data Source: Final Lower Tuolumne Instream Flow Study Report (pg. 108-109)

Instream spawning and rearing habitat for Fall Run Chinook Salmon and Steelhead in the Tuolumne River is based on data from a California Department of Fish and Game site specific study. This data consists of 10 sites spread over 9.2 miles of the dominant spawning reach of the Lower Tuolumne River and was reviewed by Stillwater Sciences for use in their 2014 Flo2D model. See original data in tables H-1 and H-2 on pgs 108-109 of Final Lower Tuolumne Instream Flow Study Report (linked above). The instream spawning and rearing habitat data was provided to us by Mark Gard from the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service in a spreadsheet (see ‘~/cvpiaHabitat/data-raw/mark_gard_data/IFIMWUA.xlsx’). Mark Gard instructed us to use cells A2:H32 in the ‘Tuolumne’ tab within his ‘IFIMWUA.xlsx’.
